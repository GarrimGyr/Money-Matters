How do Remittances Change Lives?

Daniel Pratama, McKenzie Humann, John Devine



















We want to look at the difference between families that received remittances and not. To do that, we map the result of the survey responses initiated between WFP and the International Organization for Migration (IOM) by plotting the distribution of percentage of families that received remittances per total respondents at municipality level.





































From the map, we can see that the distribution of families that received remittances differ greatly from municipality to municipality. Out of 151 municipalities interviewed, there are 17 municipalities where more than 50% of families received remittances. This implies that the majority of families across municipalities are not receiving remittances. However, for those families that do receive remittances, the impact is quite significant.





































Looking at only families that have had a family member migrate, those that receive remittances are more likely to have savings, but also are more likely to have debt.
